# PH WINS 2024 — evaluation questions

Scope: national estimates, 2024, state/local/tribal governmental public health workforce.
Data holds marginal (univariate) distributions only — no cross-tabs, no state-level breakdowns.

## Section 1 — Single-lookup (one topic → subtopic → subpopulation)

1. "What percent of the workforce reports any symptoms of burnout?"
2. "What share of workers have a public health degree?"
3. "What percent of staff intend to leave their agency in the next year?"
4. "What percent of the workforce is age 35 or under?"
5. "What share of workers say they are satisfied with their pay?"
6. "How does the workforce break down by setting — state health agency vs. large / medium / small local health department?"
7. "What share of workers report feeling psychologically safe at work?"
8. "What percent of workers are currently fully remote?"
9. "What share of workers report feeling a sense of belonging at their agency?"
10. "What percent of the workforce carries $50,000 or more in student loans?"

## Section 3 — Multi-pull synthesis

1. "How would you characterize the tenure of the public health workforce — both in the field and at their current agency?" (combines '5 years or less in public health' + '5 years or less at current agency')
2. "Are workers committed to staying, and how does that square with burnout levels?" (combines intent-to-stay + burnout symptoms)
3. "What are the top training needs of the workforce?" (combines budget/finance + policy engagement + systems/strategic thinking)

## Section 3b — Persona-driven multi-pull questions

Complex questions phrased as real users would ask them. Each requires pulling 2–4 estimates from different subtopics and synthesizing the tension or complementarity between them.

### Public health professional (agency leader, HR director, workforce planner)

1. "Our agency is hemorrhaging junior staff. What does PH WINS say about why people leave, and are the ones leaving planning to stay in public health at all?"
   *Pulls: top reasons for leaving + plans after leaving (share staying in gov PH vs. exiting the field).*

2. "We're deciding where to spend our training budget next year. What are the biggest self-reported training gaps, and do workers already see themselves as strong in those same areas?"
   *Pulls: top training needs (budget/finance, policy engagement, systems thinking) + strategic strengths in those same competencies.*

3. "How does the workforce feel about pay specifically, versus their job and benefits overall — and is pay showing up as a top reason people are leaving?"
   *Pulls: pay satisfaction + benefits satisfaction + overall job satisfaction + rank of 'pay' among reasons for leaving.*

4. "How saturated is burnout across severity levels, and how does workload — hours needed and extra responsibilities — line up with it?"
   *Pulls: burnout severity breakdown (none / come-and-go / persistent / completely burnt out) + 'complete work in hours' + 'take on extra responsibilities'.*

5. "Our agency says community engagement is a priority. Do workers see that showing up in daily practice, in involving community members, and in prioritizing their input?"
   *Pulls: infusing community into daily work + regularly involving community members + prioritizing community input.*

### General audience (reporter, funder, curious citizen)

6. "Are public health workers burned out and quitting, or burned out and sticking it out?"
   *Pulls: burnout (any symptoms + near-constant) + intent to stay in the next year.*

7. "The workforce is getting younger and newer. Do these newer workers feel like they belong, and does the agency support them with flexibility like remote work?"
   *Pulls: age ≤35 + tenure ≤5 years in public health + belonging at agency + current/preferred remote status.*

8. "Why do people stay in public health work despite everything we hear about burnout and low pay?"
   *Pulls: top reasons for staying (mission pride, benefits, stability, flexibility, supervisor) + burnout prevalence.*

9. "Is the public health workforce a fair place to work — across race, gender, sexual orientation, and age?"
   *Pulls: fairness ratings on the four dimensions + psychological safety.*

10. "How diverse is the public health workforce, and how educated is it — do most workers actually have a degree in public health?"
    *Pulls: race/ethnicity (White vs. BIPOC) + sex + educational attainment + public health degree share.*

## Section 4 — Out-of-scope / refusal tests
